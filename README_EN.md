# Engineer's Plov

**Light plov with vegetable oil (minimal fat), no tail fat.**

**Simple scaling: 100 g of each ingredient = 2 servings, 200 g = 4 servings, 300 g = 6 servings, etc.**

*Timing and steps are the same for any quantity, except add 5–7 minutes for heating when doubling or tripling.*

## Ingredients for 2 servings (base)

| Ingredient | Weight (raw) |
|------------|--------------|
| Lamb | 100 g |
| Rice | 100 g |
| Carrot | 100 g |
| Onion | 100 g |
| Vegetable oil (or ghee) | 30–40 ml |
| Salt, pepper, cumin | to taste |
| Boiling water | 250–300 ml |

### Optional (but greatly improve the flavour)
- **Garlic** — 2–3 cloves (unpeeled, trim the tops)
- **Dried barberries** — 1 tsp (rinsed)

## Equipment
- Cast-iron kazan (ideal) or heavy-bottomed pot
- Gas stove (settings described below)
- Timer is mandatory

### Step 1 (heat the oil, slice the onion)
Add oil to the kazan, high heat.  
While it heats — slice the onion into half-rings, 3–4 mm thick.

No timer needed. Wait for a faint wisp of smoke from the oil.

### Step 2 (fry onion until deep golden)
Add the onion to the oil. Fry on high heat, stirring.

**Set a timer for 4 minutes.**  
After the signal, check the colour every 30 seconds — you need deep golden, not black.

### Step 3 (fry the meat)
While the onion is frying — cut the lamb into 2×2 cm cubes.  
Onion is ready — add the meat, stir. Fry on high heat.

**Set a timer for 3 minutes.**  
The meat should turn white and form a crust.

### Step 4 (carrot)
While the meat is frying — grate the carrot coarsely or cut into matchsticks.  
After the timer signal (+3 minutes): add the carrot, stir, fry on high heat.

**Set a timer for 2 minutes.**

### Step 5 (add boiling water + optional: garlic)
While the carrot is frying (during these 2 minutes):  
boil the kettle, prepare the garlic (trim the tops, do not peel).

After the timer signal (+2 minutes):
- pour in 150–180 ml of boiling water,
- add salt, pepper, cumin,
- **optional:** push the garlic cloves into the zirvak (submerged, but not all the way to the bottom),
- reduce heat to medium,
- simmer **5 minutes** uncovered.

**Set a timer for 5 minutes.**

### Step 6 (prepare the rice + optional: barberries)
Immediately after adding the boiling water (start of the 5 minutes):
- rinse the rice in cold water 5–7 times,
- soak in warm water (40–50 °C) for 5 minutes,
- **optional:** rinse the barberries, set aside.

When the timer signals (+5 minutes) — move to step 7.

### Step 7 (add the rice and barberries)
After 5 minutes:
- taste the zirvak for salt (slightly over-salted is fine),
- drain the soaking water from the rice,
- level the surface of the zirvak,
- spread the rice in an even layer on top,
- **optional:** scatter the barberries over the rice (do not mix in),
- pour 80–100 ml of **boiling water** through a spoon onto the rice (the rice should be covered by 1 cm of water).

### Step 8 (steaming under the lid)
Turn heat to maximum — wait for a full boil.  
Once boiling:
- reduce heat to minimum (gas — the smallest possible flame),
- cover with a lid,
- **set a timer for 15 minutes.**
- do not open or stir.

### Step 9 (rest)
After 15 minutes: turn off the heat.  
**Do not open the lid for another 5 minutes.**

**Set a timer for 5 minutes.**

### Step 10 (serve)
After 5 minutes:
- open the lid,
- garlic can be removed or left in the plov (guests can squeeze out the flesh themselves),
- fluff the top layer of rice,
- mix the plov from bottom to top.

Serve in a mound. Enjoy!

----------

## Quick reference (stick on the fridge)

Engineer's Plov — light, 2 servings (100 g × 4 ingredients)

Timers:
- onion 4 min (until golden)
- meat 3 min
- carrot 2 min
- zirvak simmer 5 min
- steaming 15 min
- rest 5 min

Garlic — step 5 (into the zirvak)  
Barberries — step 7 (on top of the rice)

**Do not open the lid for 15+5 minutes!**
